What We Actually Look at Before Running a Single Ad

Most agencies start with the creative. Pick some images, write a headline, set a budget, and launch. Then they spend weeks optimizing ads that were never set up to convert in the first place.

We do things differently. At InkLock, we refuse to turn on a single ad until the backend is ready. Here's what that actually looks like.

The Landing Page

Where is the traffic going? If the answer is your homepage, we have a problem. A dedicated landing page with a single call to action, clear messaging, and a fast load time is the minimum. We check mobile responsiveness, form functionality, and whether the page actually loads in under three seconds. If the destination can't convert, the traffic is wasted.

The CRM and Lead Routing

When someone fills out a form, what happens next? We need to see the lead appear in your CRM, get tagged properly, trigger a notification to the right person on your team, and kick off an automated follow-up sequence. If any of those steps are missing or broken, leads will fall through the cracks before a human ever touches them.

Tracking and Attribution

If you can't measure it, you can't optimize it. We verify that your Meta Pixel, Google Tag, and conversion events are all firing correctly before launch. We also set up UTM parameters so you know exactly which campaign, ad set, and creative drove each lead. Without clean tracking, you're spending money blind.

The Follow-Up Sequence

Most leads don't convert on the first touch. They need nurturing. Before we launch, we build out at least a five-touch follow-up sequence that includes an immediate confirmation email, a value-driven follow-up within 24 hours, and a series of touchpoints over the next two weeks. Speed to lead matters, but consistency matters more.

Budget Alignment

We run the math backward. What's your average client value? What close rate can your team sustain? What cost per lead does the market support? Those numbers determine the budget, not the other way around. If the math doesn't work at your budget, we'll tell you that before you spend a dollar.

This is why we say marketing is the destination, not the starting point. The backend has to be ready first. Once it is, the ads have something to feed into, and the results follow.
