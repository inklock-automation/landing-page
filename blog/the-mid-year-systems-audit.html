The mid-year systems audit: 12 questions worth an hour

By InkLock Team · June 28, 2026 · 6 min read
The short version

Halfway through the year is the perfect time to honestly check your systems. Twelve questions across lead capture, follow-up, pipeline, and process. Answer them honestly, fix the worst one.

Somehow we're already halfway through the year. Wild. Before everyone sprints into the back half, I want to give you something genuinely useful. Twelve honest questions about your systems. Set aside one quiet hour, no distractions, and actually answer them. Not the answer you wish were true. The real one.

This is the same kind of thing we do at the start of any engagement, because you can't fix what you won't look at directly. So let's look. Four areas, three questions each.

Lead capture: are you catching what you paid for?

  1. When a lead comes in, where does it go? Can you trace the exact path, automatically, every time? Or does it depend on someone checking an inbox?
  2. How fast does a new lead get a response? Be honest, and measure the slow days too, not just the good ones. If it's hours, that's leaking money.
  3. Are you capturing lead source automatically? Do you actually know which channel produces clients, or are you guessing because the data's a mess?

If these answers are shaky, you're paying to generate leads and then losing them at the door. That's the most expensive leak there is.

Follow-up: does it run without you?

  1. Does every new lead get a follow-up sequence automatically? Or does follow-up only happen when someone remembers?
  2. What happens to a lead who goes quiet? Is there a re-engagement path, or do cold leads just vanish into the database forever?
  3. What happens to a no-show? Is there an automatic, graceful path back, or does a missed call just become a dead lead?

Follow-up that depends on memory is follow-up that fails on your busy weeks, which are exactly the weeks it matters most.

Pipeline and CRM: can you trust the number?

  1. Does your pipeline reflect reality? Or is it full of stale deals that should've been closed or cleared months ago?
  2. Does your team actually use the CRM? If they don't trust it or it's a pain to update, it's not really running your business, it's just decoration.
  3. Can you get a real revenue forecast from your system right now? Without a meeting, without chasing anyone. Just look and know.

A pipeline you can't trust is worse than no pipeline, because it makes you confident about the wrong things.

Process and team: does the business run without you?

  1. How much of how your business works lives only in your head? If you were out for two weeks, what breaks?
  2. Are your core processes documented and tested? Not "kind of." Could a brand new person follow them and succeed?
  3. Where do things fall through the cracks most often? You already know the answer. That spot is your most important fix.

What to do with your answers

If a bunch of those answers made you wince, good. That means you looked honestly, and honest is the only useful way to do this. It does not mean your business is failing. Almost every growing business has gaps here, because systems get outgrown faster than they get rebuilt.

Here's the move. Do not try to fix all twelve. That's how people get overwhelmed and fix none. Pick the single worst answer. The one question whose answer genuinely worries you most. That's your back-half priority. Fix that one thing properly before you touch anything else.

One real fix beats twelve half-fixes, every time. The whole back half of your year gets better if you go into it having closed your biggest gap instead of ignoring it. So take the hour. Answer honestly. Then go fix the worst one. That's the entire assignment, and it's a genuinely good one.

Frequently asked questions

What is a mid-year systems audit?

A mid-year systems audit is an honest review of your business systems across four areas: lead capture, follow-up, pipeline, and process. It uses twelve questions answered in about one hour.

What should I check in a systems audit?

Check whether leads can be traced automatically, how fast leads get a response, whether follow-up runs without you, whether the pipeline reflects reality, and how much of the business lives only in your head.

What do I do after a systems audit?

Do not try to fix everything. Pick the single worst answer, the one that worries you most, and fix that one system properly. One real fix beats a dozen half-fixes.

Why do a systems audit halfway through the year?

Mid-year is a checkpoint. Reviewing systems before the back half means you enter it having closed your biggest gap instead of carrying it through another six months.
