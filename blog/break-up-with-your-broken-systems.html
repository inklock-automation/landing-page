Break Up With Your Broken Systems

Happy Valentine's Day. While everyone else is celebrating their relationships, we're here to talk about the one you need to end: the toxic relationship you have with your business systems.

You know the ones. The CRM you paid for but never configured. The project management tool your team abandoned after two weeks. The spreadsheet that was supposed to be "temporary" three years ago and now runs half your operations.

The Sunk Cost Trap

The most common reason businesses stick with broken systems is because they already paid for them. "We spent $5,000 setting this up, we can't just walk away." Yes, you can. And you should. The money is already gone. What you're losing now is time, leads, and sanity every single day you keep using something that doesn't work.

Signs Your Systems Need to Go

Your team has workarounds for the workarounds. When people build side processes to compensate for the tool that was supposed to handle the process, that's not adaptation. That's a red flag. If your team is exporting data from one tool, reformatting it in a spreadsheet, and then importing it into another tool, your systems aren't talking to each other and they never will without intervention.

What Replacement Actually Looks Like

You don't need to rip everything out at once. Start by identifying the one system that causes the most friction daily. Map out what it's supposed to do vs. what it actually does. Then evaluate whether it needs to be reconfigured, replaced, or eliminated entirely. Sometimes the answer is that you don't need that tool at all.

The Freedom on the Other Side

Businesses that invest in getting their systems right describe the same feeling: relief. Things stop falling through the cracks. The team stops complaining. Reporting becomes possible. And suddenly, you have the data and the bandwidth to actually grow instead of just survive.

So this Valentine's Day, do yourself a favor. Break up with the systems that aren't working. You deserve better.
