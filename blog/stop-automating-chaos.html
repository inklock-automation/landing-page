New lead comes in? Automate it. Client onboarding? Automate it. Invoicing, scheduling, follow-ups? Automate all of it. And that sounds great until you realize you're automating a mess. Automation Amplifies What Already Exists If your onboarding process is confusing for clients when it's manual, automating it doesn't make it less confusing. It just makes the confusion happen faster and at scale. Automation is a multiplier, and multipliers work in both directions. The Right Order of Operations Before you automate anything, document the current process. What are the steps? Who's responsible for each one? Where do things break down? You'll almost always find bottlenecks, redundancies, or steps that shouldn't exist at all. Fix those first. Then automate the clean version. Start With the Highest-Impact, Lowest-Risk Workflow Don't start by automating your most complex sales pipeline. Start with something repeatable and straightforward, like a welcome email sequence or a client intake form that creates a task in your project management tool. Get a win. Build confidence. Then tackle the bigger stuff. The Question to Ask Before Every Automation Before building any automation, ask: "If this runs 100 times without anyone checking it, will it produce good outcomes?" If the answer is anything other than a confident yes, the process isn't ready to be automated yet. At InkLock, we don't start with the tools. We start with the process.
