5 Zapier Automations Every Small Business Owner Needs

You don't need 50 automations. You don't need a complicated 47-step workflow that connects every tool in your tech stack. You need five good ones that save you real time every single week.

These are the five we set up for almost every client we work with, regardless of industry. They're simple, reliable, and they pay for themselves within the first month.

1. New Lead Instant Notification

Trigger: Someone fills out your contact form or books a call.
Action: Instant Slack message or text notification to you and your team, plus the lead gets added to your CRM with the right tags and pipeline stage.

Speed to lead is everything. The business that responds first wins the deal. This automation makes sure no lead sits unnoticed for hours.

2. Welcome Email Sequence Trigger

Trigger: New contact added to CRM.
Action: Kicks off a 5-email welcome sequence introducing your services, sharing a case study, and inviting them to book a call.

Most leads aren't ready to buy the moment they find you. This sequence keeps you top of mind without you lifting a finger.

3. Client Onboarding Packet

Trigger: Deal marked as "Won" or invoice paid.
Action: Automatically sends welcome email, intake form, onboarding doc, and creates a project in your PM tool with all the standard tasks pre-loaded.

No more scrambling to remember what to send. Every new client gets a consistent, professional experience from day one.

4. Missed Follow-Up Alert

Trigger: A deal in your pipeline hasn't been updated in 5 days.
Action: Sends you a reminder with the contact's details and last activity.

Deals don't die because the prospect said no. They die because you forgot to follow up. This one catches the ones slipping through.

5. Weekly KPI Summary

Trigger: Every Monday at 8am.
Action: Pulls your key numbers (new leads, deals closed, revenue, ad spend) and sends a formatted summary to your inbox or Slack.

Start every week knowing exactly where you stand without logging into five different dashboards.

That's it. Five automations. No complexity, no maintenance headaches, just real time saved. And once these are running, you'll start seeing the bigger opportunities for automation across your business.
