Count the clicks between 'interested' and 'booked'

By InkLock Team · August 16, 2026 · 5 min read
The short version

Every step between someone saying yes and actually booking is a place to lose them. Walk your own path like a stranger, count the clicks and the dead air, and cut everything that isn't pulling its weight.

People don't quit because they stopped wanting it

They quit because the path got annoying. A form that asks for their whole life story. A "we'll get back to you" with no hint of when. A booking link buried three emails deep. Every extra step quietly leaks people who were genuinely ready to move. They didn't lose interest. They lost patience.

Be a stranger to your own funnel

Open an incognito window and start exactly where a real lead starts. Now count. Every click, every form field, every "thanks, someone will reach out," every stretch of silence, all the way from "I'm interested" to "I'm booked." Most owners are genuinely shocked at how long their own path is. Your marketing did its job and got someone to the door. Then the backend made them stand out in the rain while you fumbled for the keys.

Where the friction usually hides

  • The form that asks for everything up front. You don't need their company size and budget to start a conversation. Get the easy yes, ask the rest later.
  • The handoff with no confirmation. Silence after someone submits reads as "did that even work?" A simple "got it, here's what happens next" holds them.
  • The reply-to-schedule loop. Sending a real booking link beats "what times work for you?" and the three-day email tennis match that follows.
  • The speed gap. An hour of dead air after a hot lead comes in is friction too. Interest cools fast.
You spent real money getting someone to "yes." Don't lose them to a clunky form and a three-day silence.

Marketing fills the top. Systems decide who makes it out the bottom.

The slickest ad in the world can't outrun a broken booking path. This is why we won't run marketing onto a backend that can't convert: you'd just be paying to pour more people into a leaky bucket. Tighten the path and the exact same traffic books more. Nothing about your ads or your offer changed. You just stopped leaking the people you already won.

So count the clicks. Cut the ones that aren't earning their place. Make the trip from "yes" to "booked" the easiest thing you ask anyone to do all day. Simple as that.

Frequently asked questions

What causes friction in a booking process?

Usually too many form fields up front, no confirmation after a submission, back-and-forth scheduling instead of a direct booking link, and slow response times. Each one quietly loses people who were ready to move.

How do I find the friction in my funnel?

Walk it yourself in an incognito window, starting where a real lead starts. Count every click, field, and wait from 'interested' to 'booked.' The slow or confusing steps will be obvious once you're in a stranger's shoes.

How many steps should booking take?

As few as you can manage. Capture just enough to start the conversation, confirm immediately, and offer a direct way to book. Every extra step you remove tends to lift conversion.

Is booking friction a marketing or systems problem?

Both, which is exactly why they can't be solved separately. Marketing brings people to the door; the backend systems decide how many actually make it through. Fix the path and your existing traffic converts better with no extra spend.
