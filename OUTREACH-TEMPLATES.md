# Site Health Audit — Outreach Templates

Email templates for offering the Site Health Audit to prospects. Pick the one that matches the relationship.

## Template A: Cold outreach (someone you don't know well)

**Subject:** Quick question about how AI finds [Company Name]

```
Hi [Name],

I built a Site Health Audit tool that scores any website on three pillars:

- Traditional SEO (how Google ranks you)
- AEO (how voice assistants and featured snippets read you)
- GEO (how ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini decide whether to cite you)

Most agencies focus on #1 and ignore the other two. That's expensive. AI search is already pulling traffic away from Google for a third of B2B searches and that number's growing fast.

I ran my tool on my own site to test it — went from F (38/100) to A (99/100) in 48 hours of focused fixes. Then ran it on a partner brand and pulled them from F (57) to A (94) in the same playbook.

I'd like to run a free baseline scan on [companyname.com] this week. Takes me ~3 minutes to run, no setup on your end, and you'll get a real dashboard back showing:

- Your score on each pillar
- The top 3 fixes that would move the needle most
- A short note on what to prioritize

Worst case you learn something. Best case we figure out if there's a fit to work together.

Want me to run it? Just reply with "yes."

— Erin
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific, technical, but not jargon-heavy
- Real receipts (F→A numbers)
- Free baseline is the hook
- "Just reply yes" — minimal friction
- Tone matches her voice (no fluff, no AI buzzwords, no em dashes)

## Template B: Warm outreach (past client, referral, network)

**Subject:** Mind if I run something on [company]?

```
Hey [Name],

Hope [project / family / business] is good.

I built a thing this week — a site audit tool that scores websites for traditional SEO, voice search (AEO), and AI search (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini). Most audit tools score one of those three. Mine does all three with a published methodology so you can verify every finding.

I'm building case studies and want to run it on a handful of sites I trust. Mind if I run it on [theircompany.com] this week? Takes 3 minutes on my end, no setup on yours. You get a dashboard back showing where you score, the top 3 fixes, and my honest take.

No pitch attached. If something interesting comes back I'll tell you. If everything's already optimized I'll tell you that too.

Cool if I run it?

— Erin
```

**Why this works:**
- Casual ask, no salesperson-y energy
- Frames it as "I'm building, want to help me?" not "I have a service to sell you"
- "No pitch attached" defuses the resistance
- Real "case study" framing creates social pressure to participate

## Voice notes (Erin's voice)

- Numerals for counts/dollars/days
- No em dashes (use hyphens or periods)
- Casual swears OK but sparingly — most prospect emails stay clean
- No corporate AI buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "elevate", "unlock", "transform")
- Bold section breaks every 2-4 paragraphs in longer content
- Lead with the receipt or the value, not the credential
- End with a low-friction CTA ("reply yes", "want me to run it?")
